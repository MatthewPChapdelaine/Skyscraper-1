# üè¢ Building a Tall Building

## What This Is

This is a computer program that makes a **very tall building** on your computer! It's like drawing with building blocks, but the computer does it for you.

## What You Need

- A computer program called **Blender** (ask a grown-up to help install it)
- This special building code

## How to Make Your Building

### Step 1: Get Ready
Ask a grown-up to help you open the computer program.

### Step 2: Make the Building
The computer will draw the building for you. It takes a few minutes.

### Step 3: Look at Your Building!
You can see the building on the screen. It's **black and shiny** like a mirror!

## What the Building Has

Your building has:
- **50 floors** - That's like 50 pancakes stacked up!
- **Elevators** - Like the ones at the mall
- **Stairs** - For going up and down
- **Windows** - To see outside
- **Walls** - Black and shiny

## Fun Facts

- The building is as tall as **200 meters** - that's really, really tall!
- It would take a long time to climb all the stairs
- The building has special rooms for machines
- Real buildings look like this!

## What You Can Change

You can ask a grown-up to help you:
- Make it taller or shorter
- Make it bigger or smaller
- Change the colors

## Pictures in Your Head

Imagine a really tall black building that shines in the sun. It has lots of windows that you can see through. Inside, there are elevators that go up and down, and stairs for people to use. At the top, there are machines that make the building work.

## Why This is Cool

- You made a building on the computer!
- Real architects (people who design buildings) use programs like this
- You can learn about how buildings work
- It's fun to see the building appear on the screen!

## Words You Learned

- **Blender**: A computer program for making 3D things
- **Floor**: One level of a building
- **Elevator**: A box that moves up and down to carry people
- **Architect**: A person who designs buildings
- **3D**: Three-dimensional - it looks real, like you could touch it

## Remember

- Always ask a grown-up for help with the computer
- Be patient - making the building takes time
- It's okay if you don't understand everything
- Have fun learning!
